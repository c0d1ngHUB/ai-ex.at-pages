Clear limits instead of big promises

What the assistant can do in a pilot. And what it deliberately does not do.

✓

Included in the pilot

  • Take calls within an agreed scope
  • Capture contact details, requests and call-back preferences
  • Hand over information as a call-back note
  • Forward defined cases to people
×

Deliberately excluded

  • Diagnoses, treatment or emergency decisions
  • Autonomous decisions when uncertain
  • Binding appointment scheduling without agreed rules
  • Unclear storage or sharing of call data

Data & safety

Technology supports the solution. It is not the centre of attention.

Before the pilot project, data flows, storage locations, access and handovers are agreed in writing. Local or controlled infrastructure is an option when sensitive information should not leave the company unnecessarily.

Standard: clear AI disclosure, no recording without explicit agreement, and a defined fallback path for telephony, internet or system outages.

01

AI disclosure from the start

Callers learn early that they are speaking with an AI and how their request will be handed over.

02

No recording by default

Calls are not recorded by default. Any exception is agreed explicitly in advance and only where legally appropriate.

03

Clear human responsibility

Borderline, non-approved requests and disruptions follow an agreed handover or fallback route.

04

Documented data flows

Processing, storage locations, access and deletion concepts are defined specifically before the pilot project.
